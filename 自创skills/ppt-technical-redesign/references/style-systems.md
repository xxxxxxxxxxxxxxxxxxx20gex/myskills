# Style Systems

Use a style system to create deck consistency. Style should support the content, not replace it.

## Blue-White Research Technical

Best for: AI research, algorithms, university project books, technical route pages, experiment summaries.

Visual cues:
- White or very light blue background.
- Deep blue headings, cyan connectors, small orange or green evidence highlights.
- Thin grid lines, minimal shadows, paper-figure clarity.
- Diagrams should look precise, not decorative.

Avoid:
- Large dark gradients.
- Excessive icons.
- Product-marketing hero composition.

## Dark Medical Trust

Best for: healthcare AI, diagnosis assistance, Chinese medicine, privacy, traceability, safety review, expert audit.

Visual cues:
- Deep emerald, charcoal green, or blue-black background.
- Restrained gold for sequence numbers, evidence labels, and final conclusions.
- Glass panels with thin green borders.
- Evidence chain, audit panel, knowledge graph nodes, safety boundary boxes.

Avoid:
- Mystical Chinese medicine decoration.
- Claims that imply replacing doctors.
- Unreadable small text on dark backgrounds.

## Black-Gold Governance

Best for: security, compliance, risk control, permission systems, trustworthy AI, auditability.

Visual cues:
- Dark base, gold accents, thin divider lines.
- Control loops, review gates, risk-level chips, audit logs.
- Formal hierarchy and conservative spacing.

Avoid:
- Full-page gold gradients.
- Decorative shields without real control logic.
- Alarm-style red unless the page is explicitly about risk.

## Industrial Blue-Gray Engineering

Best for: industrial software, edge computing, device networks, scheduling systems, digital twins.

Visual cues:
- Blue-gray base, equipment icons, state lights, pipelines, topology maps.
- KPI blocks for uptime, throughput, latency, error rate, capacity.
- Clear distinction between device, edge, cloud, platform, and operator layers.

Avoid:
- Consumer SaaS softness.
- Random circuit decoration that does not map to system structure.

## Government / Enterprise Project Report

Best for: government proposals, state-owned enterprise reports, acceptance reports, construction outcomes.

Visual cues:
- Stable blue, low-saturation red or gold accents, structured result strips.
- Formal section labels, construction path, guarantee system, measurable outcomes.
- High orderliness and strong alignment.

Avoid:
- Cyberpunk styling.
- Overly playful icons.
- Casual marketing slogans.

## Startup SaaS Product

Best for: product capability pages, customer-facing demos, operation dashboards, platform overviews.

Visual cues:
- Clean dark or light UI panels, dashboard cards, product workflow, role-based views.
- Short value labels, user task grouping, crisp interface metaphors.
- Use accent colors for active modules and key outcomes.

Avoid:
- Making every page a dashboard.
- Fake metrics that were not provided.
