# Review Checklist

Use before finalizing a slide rewrite, layout spec, or PPTX edit.

## Message

- The slide answers one clear question.
- The title states the core message, not just the topic.
- The subtitle adds scenario, method, or boundary instead of repeating the title.
- No unsupported metric, module, or claim was invented.

## Structure

- The main visual form matches the content type.
- The reading order is obvious.
- Arrows represent real flow, dependency, or causality.
- Repeated elements are aligned and visually consistent.

## Technical Specificity

- Labels use project-specific nouns.
- Inputs, outputs, constraints, methods, or evidence are visible where relevant.
- Risks, assumptions, and boundaries are marked when they matter.
- Generic words such as "platform", "empower", "intelligent", and "efficient" are replaced with concrete functions or outcomes.

## Visual Quality

- One main object dominates the page.
- Accent color is used sparingly.
- Text is readable at presentation distance.
- Dense content is grouped into lanes, panels, rows, or modules.
- Decoration does not compete with content.

## Delivery

- If only giving advice, include a concrete layout and rewritten copy.
- If editing PPTX, verify with rendering or explain why rendering could not be run.
- If using generated images, warn that final Chinese text should be checked or overlaid in PPT when density is high.
